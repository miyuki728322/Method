How to Wash 研ぐ
01
The first water, fast
Add cold water, swirl three or four times, and drain at once. The grain drinks fastest on first contact — don't let it sit in dusty water.
02
Gentle turns, no force
Cup your hand and turn with light pressure — the togi motion — ten to fifteen rotations. You are rinsing, not grinding.
03
Repeat until translucent
Fresh water, turn, drain — two or three rounds — until cloudy-translucent, not perfectly clear. Over-washing strips flavour.
04
Drain, then rest
Let the damp grain stand so water reaches the core. By season: ~30 min in summer, up to an hour in winter when cold water soaks in slowly.
Water Quality 軟水
Use soft water — under 60 mg/L. Cooked rice is more than half water by weight, so water is an ingredient. Hard water's calcium and magnesium bind the grain and slow even absorption — rice cooks firmer and drier, its sweetness muted. Use filtered or bottled soft water, and soak in it too: the grain drinks most during the rest.
The Water Ratio 計量
New crop (shinmai)1 : 1.10–1.15Fresh rice holds its own moisture — pull water back.
Standard modern stock1 : 1.20The reliable baseline. Start here, adjust by result.
Older / drier stock1 : 1.20–1.25Dried-out grain drinks more to reach the same core.
Sushi rice (firmer)1 : 1.00–1.10Cook firm — seasoned vinegar adds moisture after.
Low-amylose (Milky Queen)1 : 1.00–1.05Already soft and sticky; less water keeps grains intact.
Weigh, don't measure by volume — cups drift 15%+. Seasonal trim: summer −2–3% with a shorter soak; winter +2–3% and a longer cold soak.
How to Cook 炊く
01
Weigh, wash, rest 30 min, then weigh the water to your chosen ratio.
02
Lid on. Medium-high to a boil over ~5 min, then drop to low and simmer 12–13 min. Do not lift the lid.
03
Off the heat — steam. Stand covered a full 10 min; residual steam finishes the core and sets the texture.
04
Fluff and release. Fold with a cut-and-turn motion — shari-kiri — so every grain stays separate and glossy.
Old rule: はじめチョロチョロ、中パッパ — start low, then a strong middle boil. The curve, not the clock alone.
Discipline
Honour the rest
The 10-min lid-on steam is cooking, not waiting. Plate early and the centre stays watery.
Lid never lifts mid-cook
Each peek dumps steam and heat; rice cooks unevenly. Trust the curve and timer.
Respect the heat curve
Hard boil to start, gentle simmer to finish. Rhythm beats raw power.
Fluff immediately
Cut and turn the moment the rest ends, or the packed base steams to mush.
Common Failures
Gummy & pasty
Cause: surface starch left on, or rice crushed. Fix: rinse gently to translucent.
Hard, chalky core
Cause: under-hydrated. Fix: rest 30 min; nudge ratio up.
Scorched base
Cause: heat too high, too long. Fix: lower the flame; trust residual steam.
Wet & collapsing
Cause: too much water, or new crop on an old ratio. Fix: pull water back 5%.
